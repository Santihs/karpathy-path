## What clicked
- mat2vec / Mat-as-Vec: matrix and vector are structurally the same thing (dict of label→value); mat2vec is a free reinterpretation (same `.f` dict), not a copy — same idea as `.view(-1)` on a PyTorch tensor
- Dot-product is a general "weighted sum → scalar" tool; downsampling's 1/16 weights are just one choice of weights, not a property of dot-product itself
- Needle-in-haystack: highest dot-product score isn't always the best match when the pattern has zero entries — computed the theoretical max score, spotted the false positive
- Triangularity depends entirely on chosen row/col order, not intrinsic to the data — `pp()` and `to_str()` share the same underlying `_table` helper, only the label order differs
- Found and fixed a real transcription bug (swapped `@`/`#` values copying the book's matrix) via a property-based test failing — reinforced why testing the math property beats testing against a hardcoded string
- End-to-end: `find_triangular_order` + `triangular_solve` (Cap 2.11, already implemented) actually solves a 3x3 system, not just detects structure
